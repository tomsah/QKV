Dancer bios

Quang Kien Van

Quang Kien Van trained at Central School of Ballet in London and went on to dance for a wide variety of companies including Adventures in Motion Pictures, Skanes Danstheater, Peter Schaufuss Ballet and Cirque du Soleil. As a UK based freelance dancer, he has worked with choreographers such as Michael Clark, Michael Keegan Dolan, Jacky Lansley, Fleur Darkin, Linda Dobell and Alex Reynolds across Theatre, Film and Opera productions. In the spring of 2013 Quang launched "Portraits of Dancers" a series of dance videos that reflect his ongoing research into the dancing body as artist, performer, storyteller, communicator of ideas.

Rachele Rapisardi

After graduating from Rotterdam Conservatory (CODARTS) Rachele relocated to Paris where she worked closely with Nina Dipla. In 2010 she joined Edge11 directed by Jeanne Yasko which toured internationally. Rachele has since worked with Jorge Crecis, Jose Agudo (Akram Khan), Emma Martin Dance (Ireland), ilDance Company (Sweden). She is currently working in the Uk with Ben Duke (Lost Dog), Protein Dance directed by Luca Silvestrini and Gwyn Emberton.

Chihiro Kawasaki - Dancer, Aikido (Japanese martial art) and Pilates/dance instructor

Born in Kyoto, Japan. Sorely traveled to the UK at the age of 14 to study performing arts at Arts Educational School, Tring (now called Tring Park). Chihiro then went on to gain an One Year certificate in contemporary dance at London Contemporary Dance School (LCDS) and has joined Henri Oguike Dance Company (HODC) as an apprentice as a part of LCDS scheme. Soon after joining the company, she was offered to be a full time dancer at HODC and also at Phoenix Dance Theatre (PDT). CChihiro is a qualified Pilates teacher and an assistant instructor of Shodokan Aikido.

Sarita Piotrowski - Dancer

Sarita was originally born and raised in Leeds, where she trained at Northern School of Contemporary Dance. She then dedicated six years to extensive National and Global touring, for Dance companies: Henri Oguike and The National Dance Company of Wales (Diversions). In this time she became an established performer and contemporary dance artist. In 2008 Sarita embarked on a freelance career, breaking into the commercial pop dance industry. This allowed her to diversify and broaden many differing performance and dance styles, whilst also choreographing and directing her own dance projects. She has worked on many TV productions, live shows, tours and music videos for numerous mainstream music artists and celebrities such as: Take That, Cheryl Cole, Shakira, Will.i.am, La Roux, Katherine Jenkins, Pet Shop Boys and David Hayes.

In the latter years of her career she has embarked upon acting roles, appearing in HBO's ‘Game of Thrones’ TV program as well as productions at the Shakespeare’s Globe Theatre. Other credits include performances for Sadler’s Wells Theatre, Queen Elizabeth Hall, The Royal Opera House, Wembley Arena, the BBC, ITV, and multiple invitations for the 2012 Olympic Ceremonies. She has worked for and performed numerous works by choreographers such as Wayne Mc Gregor, Itzik Galili, Javier De Frutos, Stephen Petronio, Russel Maliphant, Robert Hilton, Lynne Page and Brian Friedman, to name but a few. Currently Sarita is collaborating with an array of artists, performing and developing her own catalogue of solo works whilst also founding dance company Enuki Entertaiments.

Rebecca Evans

Rebecca Evans has been moving for a long time in many places and has enjoyed working with Marc Brew Company, Ffin Dance and IJAD Dance Company. Rebecca also had fun doing Wayne Mcgregor's Dancelines 2011 and spent time in 2012 in Korea teaching, learning and working with Korean National Contemporary Dance Company. 2013 brought some new and wonderful opportunities for creations. She collaborated with Kloe Dean and Myself UK to create a hip hop contemporary piece for Collabo 2013. She premiered her company Pell Ensemble at Resolution! 2013 with the piece 'Subliminal Effects'. Rebecca also started an exciting series of video, performance art and installation experiments with Zoe Rado, forming part of the YJGF collective.

Riccardo Vitello - Dancer

Riccardo Vitello was born in Palermo, Italy. He completed his dance studies at the London Contemporary Dance School where he graduated in 2007 with a BA honours degree. He the took an apprenticeship with NDCW obtaining a PGD through LCDS. Riccardo then joined Fleur Darkin Company for the creation of “ DisGo “ and “The Blake Diptych” Innocence and Experience. In England he then worked for Nigel Charnock, The Generating Company, Mavin Khoo Dance, Roberta Jean, Diciembre Dance Group and Cedar Dance Animation and Shobana Yejasingh. In Italy he worked with Aldes/Roberto Castello, Ubidanza, Gruppo Festina Lente, Carla Onni and Elisabetta Consonni.

He has choreographed for the last couple of years. ”Make it leave it, go and get it back again” was presented last summer for a contemporary dance festival in Sicily. He also teaches contemporary dance technique for teenagers and adults in colleges in England and Italy.

Karine Rathle

Dancer, choreographer, teacher, rehearsal director and researcher in dance and dance science, Karine Rathle is also co-founder and co-director of Fleur d’asphalte, a dance and creation studio in Montreal (Canada). She has a MSc degree in Dance Science from Trinity Laban Conservatoire of Music and Dance, an internationally renowned vocational dance institute. Dance has been an integral part of Karine’s life since childhood, she developed solid base in classical ballet at Pierre Laporte College. Since then, she has extended this experience to include Contemporary dance, argentinean tango, contact improvisation, modern dance, flamenco, argentinean folkloric dances. Karine is also a Pilates certified teacher and practices yoga regularly. She has extensive knowledge in the field of Somatics and integrates it to her own practice and teaching.

Karine regularly participates as an interpreter and a choreographer in dance performances. Her work is fueled by an interest in creating a multidisciplinary, personal and authentic language. Extending beyond the perceived limits of the physical, Karine’s voyage of discovery aspires to integrate the movement, the image and the theatrical aspect in both her creation, and her performance of dance.

Ezekiel Oliveira

Ezekiel is originally from Portugal where he studied new circus and contemporary dance. He graduated from London Contemporary Dance School in 2007 and has since performed works by Tamzin Fitzgerald, Hofesh Schechter, Stephan Koplowitz, Henrique Cabrera, Helene Blackburn and Libby Seward. With Balbir Singh Dance Company he created ‘Decreasing Infinity’ (amongst other works) which is still touring internationally. He was guest dance artist for ‘DisGo’ in 2010 and in 2011 Ezekiel created ‘Blake Diptych’, Innocence and Experience in collaboration with Fleur Darkin Company. In 2012 he became the Artist in Residency at Centre Stage School of the Arts in Singapore, lecturing, creating, performing, collaborating and teaching choreography and contemporary dance.

Vanessa Abreu - Dancer

Following 12 years of ballet training, Vanessa studied in Lisbon’s Escola Superior de Dança and at Laban (BA & MA Choreography), where she receives the Simone Michelle Award for outstanding achievement in Choreography. Professional engagements and collaborators include: Shobana Jeyasingh, Upswing, Fleur Darkin Ensemble, The Generating Company, Haptic Dance, Dane Hurst, Gerrard Martin Dance, Union Dance amongst others. In 2011, Vanessa was a Southbank Emerging Artist in Residence, received a choreographic commission from En-Knap, Slovenia and was a winner of the BBC Performing Arts Fund Dance Fellowship 2011 with Upswing.

Audrey Rogero - Dancer

Originally from France, Audrey showed a strong interest for the Arts from an early age and trained both in dance and classical music at the National Conservatoire. In 2010, she received a First Class Degree from the Trinity Laban Conservatoire where she performed works by Charles Linehan, Rosalind crisp and Rosemary Butcher. At the time she was also the recipient of the Marion North Award for outstanding achievement in Performance. Since graduating Audrey worked with a range of companies including Scottish Dance Theatre, Fleur Darkin Company, Shobana Jeyasingh Dance and Dora Frankel Dance and State of Emergency.

She has performed works by Hofesh Shechter, Ben Duke, Liv Lorent, Colin Poole and Jarek Cemerek both in the UK and internationally. Alongside her performance activities, Audrey is also an enthusiastic teacher and has taught extensively classes and workshops for young professionals, young kids and mixed abled groups. Audrey is currently writing her Masters in Dance and Performance with LCDS.

Manon Greiner

Manon Greiner, born in West-Berlin, started very young with classical ballet, piano and violin. After studying history of arts, french and italian for one year she decided to study contemporary dance at the Folkwang Hochschule in Essen (Germany). One year later in 2003 she changed to Codarts in Rotterdam (Netherlands) and graduated there. In 2005 she was selected for the two years program D.A.N.C.E (Dance Apprentice Network aCross Europe) where she was working with William Forsythe, Wayne McGregor, Frédéric Flamand, Angelin Preljocaj. In 2007 she received her Master in dance from the Palucca Schule in Dresden (Germany).

She has worked with: La Fura dels Baus, Michele Anne de Mey, Thierry de Mey, William Forsythe, Angelin Preljocaj, Lutz Gregor, Pierre Droulers, Wayne McGregor, Prue Lang, Jasper Dzuki Jelen, Ezequiel Sanucci, Chuo-Tai Sun, Cesc Gelabert, Morgan Belenguer, Jean Guillaume Weis, Thomas Kopp, Rosie Kay, Alexey Kononov, Micha Purucker, Aletta Collins, Stefan Dreher, Caroline Finn, Monica Gomis, Ludger Lamers.

Sophia Preidel

Sophia was born in Germany where she competed as a gymnast, before starting her dance training at the Iwanson School for Contemporary Dance in Munich, in 2006. After graduating in 2009 Sophia moved to London. She has worked with companies such as Fleur Darkin Company, Bristol based Vex Dance Theatre, Recoil Performance Group from Denmark, Haptic Dance, Pair Dance, and most recently ACE dance and music in Birmingham.
